Agriculture matters to
the future of development

Before planting a crop, add organic matter like compost or manure, improve pH with lime or sulfur, incorporate bone meal or blood meal for nutrients, and enhance drainage with perlite or vermiculite.

Herbicides are used to control unwanted weeds in crops, but their use should be careful and responsible. Overuse can harm soil health, affect beneficial plants, and disrupt the ecosystem balance.

Water for crops often comes from wells or borewells. Wells provide surface water, while borewells tap into underground sources, offering a steady supply, especially in areas with limited rainfall or surface water.

Farming is the backbone of life; it nurtures the land, feeds the soul, and sustains communities for generations.
